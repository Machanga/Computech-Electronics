Asus ROG line up launched, starts at ksh 180,000/=

ARTICLE | BY DAVID TEPPER | APRIL 5, 2019 2:30 PM PDT

Taiwanese brand Asus has unveiled the latest range of gaming laptops under the Republic of Games (ROG) series. These include ROG Zephyrus S GX531, ROG Zephyrus S GX701, and ROG Strix SCAR II along with desktop GL12CX. While the ROG Zephyrus S GX531 is priced at Ksh 350,000 the ROG Zephyrus S GX70 comes with a price tag of Ksh 399,999. Both the devices will be available from April onwards.

The desktop GL12CX is available at Ksh 349,999. The ROG Strix SCAR II comes with a starting price of Ksh 249,000. Lastly, the Asus Hero II laptop is also priced at Ksh 254,999. All these can be purchased starting today, April 5.

This Huawei laptop aim to take on Apple's latest MacBook

ARTICLE | BY DAVID TEPPER | APRIL 5, 2019 10:54 AM PDT

Huawei at its pre-MWC 2019 conference unveiled the 2019 version of MateBook X Pro, which is targeted at jet-setting business users and professional content creators. The laptop, which has launched alongside the Huawei Mate X foldable smartphone, at pre-MWC 2019 event in Barcelona, Spain, comes as a successor to the last year’s MateBook X Pro.

Huawei’s new MateBook X Pro sports a CNC machined metallic unibody with ‘sandblasted’ finish, giving a premium look. The computing machine is available in Mystic Silver and Space Gray colour variants and is highly portable with 14.6mm thickness (max) and 4.6mm at the thinnest.

Acer unveils two new Chromebases

Acer has debuted two new Chromebases — the Acer Chromebase for Meetings 24V2 for video conferencing with Google Hangout Meet, and the Acer Chromebase 24I2, which, as per the company is an all-in-one Chrome device designed to fill multiple roles, either as an individual desktop working device, or as an in-store kiosk. Asus ZenBook 13, 14 and 15 laptops launched at a starting price of ksh 70,000

ARTICLE | BY JAMES HARRIS SIMONS | APRIL 4, 2019 9:50 PM PDT

After introducing its Zenbook 2018 line up in Taiwan back in August last year, Asus has finally brought its Zenbook 13-inch, 14-inch and 15-inch models to the Indian shelves. The firm claims these new computing machines as one of the most compact laptops in the markets right now.

While the Asus Zenbook 15 (UX533) is being touted for its ‘immersive’ 4K display, physical numeric keypad and a more lifestyle-centric design, the ZenBook 14 (UX433) and 13 (UX333) is highlighted for its compact profile, lost lasting battery backup and much more. The laptops have the iconic ‘ErgoLift’ hinge, something we have seen in other ZenBook laptops as well.
